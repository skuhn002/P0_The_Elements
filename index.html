The Elements

So what are The Elements?

    An element is nothing more than a fundamental unit. The (chemical) elements are the fundamental units that make up the world around us. Their electrical properties are each unique, because each chemical element (unique atom) has a different number of protons. Each proton is like a little magnet. Change the number of little magnets, and you change the electrical nature of the element. This unique electrical nature gives rise to the incredible living world around us, from jewlery and sea shells, to the food on your table and the water in the ocean. It's special little magnets dancing together all the way down.
